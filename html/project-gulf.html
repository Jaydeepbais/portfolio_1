Developed under Transhuman Collective to celebrate the centennial milestone of Gulf Oil, this project delivered a one of a kind permanent immersive museum experience honoring 100 years of the brand’s legacy. The installation blended over 14 traditional and emerging mediums including projection mapping, augmented reality, holography, kinetic installations, and sensor based technology to construct a rich, multi sensory brand narrative. By seamlessly intersecting art, historical legacy, and interactive engineering, the permanent exhibition transformed corporate history into a dynamic digital archive, allowing visitors to engage directly with the technology and innovation that shaped the brand over a century.

As a interactive developer and technical artist, I engineered, programmed, and deployed the core physical digital activations. This included building a proximity sensing infrastructure for the History Wall that triggered precise 3D projection mapping as visitors approached, alongside developing an interactive AR mirror that allowed guests to capture high-fidelity digital selfies with a virtual projection of brand ambassador MS Dhoni. Additionally, wired and programmed custom physical bike handle for a tangible Product Wall simulation, allowing users to accelerate dual grips to visualize real-time performance and efficiency metrics comparing Gulf Oil against its competitors.
